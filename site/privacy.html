Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Aunty (“we”, “us”) handles information when you use our website and browser games (the “Services”).

1. What we collect

We aim to collect as little as possible.

  • Gameplay data stored on your device. High scores or similar progress may be saved in your browser’s local storage. This stays on your device and is not sent to us unless a future feature clearly says otherwise.
  • Basic technical logs. Our hosting provider (for example Vercel) may automatically record standard server logs such as IP address, browser type, and request time for security and reliability.
  • No account required. You can play without creating an account or providing your name or email.

2. Cookies and similar technologies

We do not use advertising cookies. The site may use essential storage required for the games to work (such as remembering a score). If we add optional analytics later, we will update this policy and, where required, ask for consent.

3. How we use information

  • To run and improve the website and games
  • To keep the Services secure and diagnose issues
  • To understand aggregate traffic (if analytics are enabled in the future)

4. Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. Hosting and infrastructure providers process technical data only to deliver the Services on our behalf.

5. Children’s privacy

Our games are casual entertainment. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal data to us, contact us and we will delete it where applicable.

6. Your choices

  • Clear site data in your browser to remove locally stored scores
  • Stop using the Services at any time

7. Changes

We may update this Policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top will change when we do. Continued use of the Services after an update means you accept the revised Policy.

8. Contact

Questions about this Policy? Reach out via the contact method listed on the website deployment, or open an issue on the project repository if one is public.
